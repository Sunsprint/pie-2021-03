Project Goals

We started our project wanting to create something interactive that combines software complexity, opportunity for visual design, and mechanical/electrical challenges. We wanted to make something that we would be proud of at the end of the project, and most importantly, we wanted to provide a fun and cool experience for users.

We ended up deciding to make a chessboard. We wanted to be able to play single player chess against a computer as if it were a human. This meant having a physical chessboard (as opposed to a virtual one) and integrating a way for the computer to detect the state of the board and move pieces. This included using an electromagnet and a camera.

System

maybe add something here

Our project consists of several integrated systems: a 2D gantry that moved an electromagnet underneath the board, 3D printed chess pieces with magnets on the bottom, an actual chess box, and a computer AI that could see moves with a camera and control the electromagnet to move its own pieces. We focused on visual design as well as functionality, and this took the form of an arcade-like color theme and lights.

Project Origins

After discussing various ideas, we were inspired by the 2014 Harry Potter style Wizards Chess team and decided to iterate upon their POE project. We were fans of the hidden electromagnet that makes the chess pieces move on its own, but wanted to take a different approach on the visual theme. Our software team was more interested in implementing a chess AI and learning about computer vision than spending time with voice recognition technology.

Computer AI

Throughout the duration of the project, our computer chess algorithm learned how to use the camera to check where the location of the pieces and to use the stepper motor gantry system to make basic chess moves. In the future, the computer hopes to learn to make more difficult moves such as move knights, make captures, and castle so that it can win a game.
